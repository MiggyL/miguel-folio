# Sheets-to-Form Automation — Storyboard

## Segment 1 (0:00–0:09)

### Video Description
An office. A woman opens a Google Sheet — five hundred rows of employee data. Names, IDs, addresses, departments. Her job: enter each row into a web form on another tab. She copies the first cell. Switches tabs. Pastes. Clicks the next field. Copies again. The clock on the wall shows 9 AM.

### Script
"Five hundred rows of data. Each one needs to be copied from a spreadsheet into a web form — field by field, row by row. That's her entire day."

---

## Segment 2 (0:09–0:18)

### Video Description
Time-lapse. The clock spins — 10 AM, noon, 2 PM. Coffee cups pile up. Her eyes glaze over. Same motion, hundreds of times. Copy. Tab. Paste. Tab. Next field. Submit. Next row. The spreadsheet barely scrolls. She's not even halfway done.

### Script
"By 2 PM she's not even halfway. The same twelve keystrokes, five hundred times. No one thinks this can be automated. It's just how it's done."

---

## Segment 3 (0:18–0:27)

### Video Description
Screen wipe. Next morning. Same desk, same spreadsheet. But now a Chrome extension icon glows in the toolbar. She clicks it. The spreadsheet highlights row one. Data streams into the form automatically — every field fills itself. Submit. Row two. Row three. A progress bar races across. Rows fly by.

### Script
"One Chrome extension. It reads the sheet, fills every field, and submits — row after row. Five hundred entries, no human touch required."

---

## Segment 4 (0:27–0:36)

### Video Description
The progress bar finishes. All 500 rows — done. The clock shows 9:47 AM. She hasn't touched the keyboard. She picks up her coffee, still warm, and leans back. She opens her real work for the day — the work she never had time for before. She smiles.

### Script
"Done before her coffee went cold. A full day's work in forty-seven minutes. She finally has time for the work that actually matters."
