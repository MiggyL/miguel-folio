# HTTYD Telegram Bots — Storyboard

## Segment 1 (0:00–0:09)

### Video Description
A phone screen. Telegram opens. The game is called "How To Train Your AI Dragon." A menu shows different dragon characters — each with a name, a portrait, and a personality description. Toothless: loyal but guarded. Stormfly: energetic and curious. Hookfang: stubborn and proud. The player picks one.

### Script
"How To Train Your AI Dragon. You don't just pick a dragon — you pick a personality. Loyal, curious, stubborn. Each one responds differently to everything you say."

---

## Segment 2 (0:09–0:18)

### Video Description
The player messages the stubborn dragon: "Let's go flying." The dragon replies: "I don't fly with strangers. Come back when you've earned it." The player tries the curious dragon instead: "Let's go flying." It replies: "Where to? I've been wanting to explore the mountains!" Two completely different personalities, same prompt, different worlds.

### Script
"Same message, completely different reactions. The stubborn one rejects you. The curious one can't wait. These aren't scripted replies — they're personalities responding in character."

---

## Segment 3 (0:18–0:27)

### Video Description
Days later. The player returns to the stubborn dragon. Messages it again — gently this time. The dragon pauses longer than usual. Then: "...You came back. Fine. Let's fly." A trust meter fills to full. The bond is formed. Not through a button press — through patience and conversation. Fade to black.

### Script
"Days later, you come back. The stubborn one finally says yes. Not because you found a cheat code — because you kept showing up. The bond was earned through conversation."
