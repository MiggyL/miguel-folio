# Food Price Forecasting — Storyboard

## Segment 1 (0:00–0:09)

### Video Description
A busy open-air market. Vendors arranging produce — rice, vegetables, cooking oil. Families shopping with careful budgets. Price tags visible. A vendor reaches up and changes a price — higher than yesterday. A mother hesitates, puts the item back on the shelf.

### Script
"At the market, prices change without warning. A kilo of rice costs more today than last week. Families feel it first — and they have no way to prepare."

---

## Segment 2 (0:09–0:18)

### Video Description
Camera pulls back from the market into a global view. Supply chain lines pulse across a world map. A drought icon appears in Southeast Asia. A shipping delay flashes in the Pacific. The ripple effects travel — red lines reaching markets in other countries. Prices climbing everywhere.

### Script
"A drought in one country. A shipping delay in another. These events ripple across supply chains, and by the time prices spike at your local market, it's already too late."

---

## Segment 3 (0:18–0:27)

### Video Description
Transition to a dark analytics dashboard. Years of price data scroll past — a blue line charting historical food prices. Then the line continues in green — the forecast. It curves upward weeks before the actual spike. Key inflection points glow. A policy maker studies the chart, picks up a phone.

### Script
"This model studies years of price history and forecasts the spike weeks before it hits. It gives decision-makers something they never had — time."

---

## Segment 4 (0:27–0:36)

### Video Description
Back at the market. Same vendors, same families. But this time the prices are stable. The mother picks up the item confidently, places it in her basket. Full bags. The forecast chart fades quietly into the background — invisible, doing its job. Life goes on normally.

### Script
"The best prediction is the one nobody notices — because the crisis was handled before it ever reached the shelf."
